India has one billion people in a country one-fourth the size of the U.S - 329 million square kilometres of land area. Only half of it is used for agriculture. A quarter of it is degraded. Crop yields in India are are a fifth to tenth of international yields. India has varied agro-climatic zones, good rainfall and sunshine all through the year. The annual rainfall is 400 million hectare meters -- four times the annual requirement. Yet only 86% of the urban population and 69% of rural population have access to drinking water. India has large energy resources -- coal, gas-hydrates, deepwater oil and gas, hydro electric power, etc. Only a fraction of these are exploited. India uses less than 1% of it's coal reserves of 70 billion tonnes. Hydro electric power generation capacity is only one tenth of its 180,000 mega-watt potential. India has 8% of the world's biodiversity. Of the 81,000 species of fauna and 47,000 species of flora, 15,000 are unique to India.
