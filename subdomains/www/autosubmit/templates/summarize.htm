Why do we Summarize Population
and Environmental News?

The News Digest, which is on the front page of WOA!! provides a large set of news items in summarized form for quick scanning by the population-concerned and by those who may be just newly aware to the problems and impacts of overpopulation.

In addition, the WOA!! News Digest and Archives provide a valuable research tool for those who want to check out certain facts or statistics on the environment, sustainability, population, or family planning.

If you would like to help WOA!! by summarizing news articles, it is important that you retain, for each article, important statistics and facts that researchers can use. Also look for opinions from respected researchers. Many of the links to news articles go dead after awhile, but WOA!! will still retain the news summaries.

Choose from the article information which would interest the population-concerned: demographics, the environment, family planning, maternal and infant health, women's self-esteem, key political players, sustainability, etc.

When you summarize an article for WOA!!, one or two quotes are allowed, but please give credit. Show the source of the information. Cite publications so viewers can google search or go to the library and do more research. Copy a little, but paraphrase a lot, to avoid copyright infringement. Shorten paragraphs by cutting out most descriptive words and hyperbole. Cut out unclear concepts or wordy explanations. Eliminate or use anectdotal narratives sparingly.

Often the same thing will be said twice in the same article - combine or eliminate.

Do not include your own opinion except in the box provided. It will be my option to publish comments.





Publishing Sequence

A page is displayed two different ways: dynamically and statically. Generally the public sees the static page and someone working as a volunteer sees the dynamic page, which is generated on the fly so you can see your results immediately.

An example of a dynamic URL is
http://www.population-awareness.net/cgi-bin/cgiwrap/popaware/article.pl?
display_section%%%India_intro%
One drawback to dynamic pages is that images are difficult to display and we usually do not attempt to display them.

If you want to see the static page, you have to go to the location of that page - indirectly by going to the front page and navigating to the page using the indexes - or directly if you know the URL, like www.population-awareness.net/India.html

Once a webpage has been modified by a volunteer, it goes to the admin for approval, and is then moved as a static page to the public area (in otherwords, it is 'published').
